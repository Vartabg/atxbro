VetNav: Your Benefits Guide

Clear paths to the support you've earned.

Key Information: VA Health Care

  • Eligibility Basics: Generally, active military service and a non-dishonorable discharge are required.
  • Minimum Duty: Typically 24 months of continuous service or the full period called to active duty if enlisted after 9/7/80 or entered active duty after 10/16/81. Exceptions apply.
  • PACT Act Expansion: If you were exposed to burn pits, Agent Orange, or other toxins, your eligibility may be expanded. This includes many Vietnam, Gulf War, and post-9/11 era veterans.
  • No Disability Rating Needed: A service-connected disability is not required to enroll for VA health care.
  • Enrollment: VA uses priority groups to manage care access.

For full details and to apply, visit the official VA Health Care page.

Key Information: Disability Compensation

  • Service-Connected Conditions: For disabilities (illnesses/injuries) incurred or aggravated during active service. Includes physical and mental health conditions.
  • Establishing Connection: Requires 1) current diagnosed disability, 2) in-service event/illness, and 3) a medical "nexus" (link).
  • Presumptive Conditions: VA may presume service connection for certain conditions linked to specific exposures (e.g., Agent Orange, PACT Act toxic exposures) or those appearing soon after service. This can simplify proof.
  • Secondary Conditions: Compensation may also cover disabilities caused or worsened by an existing service-connected condition.
  • Discharge: Requires a discharge other than dishonorable.

For full details and to apply, visit the official VA Disability page.

Key Information: VA Pension Program

  • Purpose: Monthly payments for wartime veterans with limited income who meet specific age or disability criteria.
  • Wartime Service: Active duty for at least 90 days, with one day during a wartime period. Longer service (e.g., 24 months) usually needed if active duty began after 9/7/80.
  • Age or Disability: Must be 65+, OR have a permanent and total disability, OR be in a nursing home, OR receiving SSDI/SSI.
  • Income & Net Worth Limits: Yearly family income and net worth must be within Congressional limits. Primary home, car, and basic furnishings are usually excluded from net worth.
  • Discharge: Must be other than dishonorable.
  • Aid & Attendance/Housebound: May increase pension if you need help with daily activities or are housebound; these qualifying disabilities don't need to be service-connected.

For full details and to apply, visit the official VA Pension page.

Key Information: Education & Training

  • Overview: VA offers programs to help veterans and families pay for college, vocational training, and other education.
  • Post-9/11 GI Bill (Ch. 33): Generally for 90+ days active duty after 9/10/01 or 30 days if disability discharge. Purple Heart recipients (post-9/11) also eligible. Benefits (tuition, housing, books) tiered by service length. MHA has specific conditions (e.g., not for active duty, <1/2 time study).
  • Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB-AD): Typically needs 2+ years active duty, honorable discharge, HS diploma/GED, and initial enrollment option.
  • Other Programs: Benefits may also exist for survivors and dependents.
  • Discharge: Generally requires an honorable discharge (or under honorable for some programs).

For full details and to apply, visit the official VA Education page.

Key Information: Housing Assistance

  • Overview: VA helps veterans, service members, and surviving spouses buy, build, retain, or adapt a home.
  • VA-Backed Home Loans: VA guarantees part of loans from private lenders, often with better terms like no down payment. Needs a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) based on service history and discharge type.
  • Disability Housing Grants (SAH/SHA): For veterans with specific service-connected disabilities to buy, build, or modify homes. SAH is for severe disabilities (e.g., loss of use of both legs); SHA for others (e.g., loss of use of both hands). Max grant amounts apply (e.g., SAH FY24: $117,014; SHA FY24: $23,444).
  • HUD-VASH Program: For homeless veterans; combines HUD rental assistance with VA case management. Requires VAMC homeless determination and other criteria.
  • Disqualifiers: Generally, dishonorable discharge for loans/grants; specific disability criteria for SAH/SHA grants; lifetime sex offender registration for HUD-VASH.

For full details and to apply, visit the official VA Housing Assistance page.

Understanding the PACT Act

  • What is it? The PACT Act expands VA health care and benefits for Veterans exposed to burn pits, Agent Orange, and other toxins during service.
  • Expanded Health Care: Many Veterans who served in Vietnam, Gulf War, post-9/11 combat zones (Iraq, Afghanistan), etc., and were exposed to toxins, are now eligible for VA health care.
  • New Presumptive Conditions: Adds many new "presumptive conditions" (e.g., certain cancers, respiratory illnesses) for disability compensation if you served in qualifying locations/times. This simplifies proving service connection.
  • Why it Matters: You might now qualify for health care or disability benefits even if previously denied. If denied for a now-presumptive condition, file a supplemental claim.

Learn more and apply at the official VA PACT Act page.

Discharge Status & VA Benefits

  • Impact: Your discharge character (e.g., Honorable, OTH) is key for VA benefit eligibility. Most require a discharge not dishonorable.
  • Options if Not "Honorable":
    • VA Character of Discharge Review: VA may determine your service was "other than dishonorable" for VA purposes, potentially granting access to benefits.
    • Discharge Upgrade: You can apply to your service branch's Discharge Review Board (DRB) or Board for Correction of Military Records (BCMR).
    • Some Benefits Still Possible: Emergency mental healthcare or care for military sexual trauma may be available regardless of discharge.
  • Get Help: These processes are complex. Seek help from an accredited VSO or legal aid.

Learn more about discharge reviews and upgrades at the official VA page.
